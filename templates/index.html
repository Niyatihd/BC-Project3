Bay Area Housing Market Outlook

The american dream of owning a home continues to be a dream for many living in the bay area. Despite the record low interest rates, only 24% of people can afford to buy a median priced home. We set out to find why? Contrary to the conventional intutuion that the the supply (housing production) is not keeping up with demand demand (population growth), we found that astonomical rise in median home price in the past decade is largely fuelled by rise in high wage jobs. Based on the observed trends in households, jobs and wages, we developed a model to predict the median home prices for the nine counties - Alameda, Contra Costa, Marin, Napa, San Francisco, San Mateo, Santa Clara, Solano and Sonama - that make up the Bay Area.
